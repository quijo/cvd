Auditing Committee

  • That every local church shall adopt and implement given Chart of Accounts

Pastoral care Committee

Recommendation of Pastoral
We the members of the committee on Pastoral benefits and pension recommend the following:

  • that each local is encourage to allocate a budget for the Pastors’ benefits:
    • SSS
    • PAG-IBIG FUND
    • UTILITIES
    • COMMUNICATION
    • TRANSPORTATION
    However, if the local church fund is insufficient, the District will find a way to facilitate the need as approved by the DAB.
  • That each local church is encourage to give Honorarium, meals and travel allowance to the supply Pastor assigned by the District Superintendent and as approved by the DAB.
  • That each local church is encourage to give a 13th Month Pay, and that 60% will be given in the month of June and remaining 40% will be given in the month of December. It is also negotiable from 50% to 100% if needed and if the church can afford.
  • That each church is encourage to sponsor the assigned Pastor’s continuing education as required and recommended by the Board of Ministerial Studies. However, if the local church cannot make it, the District will find a way to facilitate the need as approved by the DAB.
  • That every local church is encourage to grant their Pastor to have a 15 day vacation with pay, annually.
  • That an incentive and / or service award be given to the Faithful Pastor by the District for their 5th, 10th, 20th year of service and beyond until the Pastor will be put to retired status. That they will also be recognize during the Annual District Assembly.
  • That all local churches are encourage to give their love gifts to our District Superintendent and family every District Assembly.
  • That the DS and DAB will meet with the local church and church board.
  • That the committee admonish our churches to carefully observe these recommendation.

Christian Action and State of the Church Committee

Recommendations of the Christian Action and State of the Church Committee

We recommend that:

  • Every Local Church is encouraged to train laypeople for teaching and preaching ministries.
  • Local Churches recognized by the Central Visayas District are encouraged to formulate a local mission, vision statement, and core values that support the district's goals and objectives. The Mission and Vision statement must be reviewed and reevaluated regularly.
  • The Articles of Faith of the Church of the Nazarene should be integrated with the Sunday School curriculum as part of the indoctrination and mentoring process.
  • The Covenant of Christian Conduct indicated in our manual should be integrated with the discipling process and experience.
  • The District Advisory Board should formulate guidelines for a modest and appropriate dress code for church workers participating in church services and ministries.
  • The local pastor set the guidelines for the use of the parsonage with the approval of the District Properties Board.
  • The local pastor is encouraged to facilitate weddings for unmarried couples with no legal impediments.
  • Every local church is encouraged to invite speakers whose doctrinal stance is consistent with the teachings of the Church of the Nazarene.
  • Churches are encouraged to give generously to guest speakers.
  • We do not support the practice of same-sex sexual intimacy and same-sex marriage.
  • We encouraged church members who have problems with illegal substances to submit to different treatment programs as a step toward proper treatment and recovery.

District Journal Committee

The journal committee recommends the following:

  • That there will be 40 copies of the journal to be produced;
    • one (1) copy each for 21 Organized churches & Mission Churches;
    • One copy each of the following: APNTS; PNC; CPNC (formerly VNBC).
    • Three for Field Office.
    • One for each district auxiliary heads.
    • The remaining journals will serve as reserve copies and to be kept by the district secretary.
  • That the budget is set at ₱6,700.00. The breakdown will be the following: Production Cost ₱5,000.00; Honorarium for workers ₱1,000.00; Mailing ₱700.00.
  • That all committee reports must be submitted to the district secretary right after the committee meeting. The reports of the auxiliary chairpersons (NYI, NMI, NDI) must be submitted to the district secretary on the day of each convention.
  • That the district secretary will have a copy of the audio recording of each of the auxiliary convention meetings.
  • That the SOFTCOPY of the journal will be submitted by the district secretary to the PMFO secretary 30 days after the district assembly through e-mail.
  • That the HARDCOPY of the journal will be submitted to the 21 organized churches and mission churches of CVD, APNTS, PNC, CPNC, District Auxiliary Heads and Field Office 60 days after the district assembly.
  • That the district secretary and all the local secretaries will take in charge of the editing, printing, distributing, and mailing of the district journal.
  • That the Journal will be at 200 pesos per copy for each local and mission church.
  • That all district licensed ministers and elders will have their personal picture (1x1) in the softcopy of the district journal.
